Safety Companion

Safety First

Daily safety tips to keep you protected in various situations

Avoid Isolated Areas at Night

Stick to well-lit, populated areas when walking at night. If you feel unsafe, call a trusted contact or use a rideshare service.

Keep Emergency Contacts Handy

Save important numbers on speed dial and also keep a written copy in your wallet in case your phone battery dies.

Secure Your Belongings

Always lock your doors and windows when leaving home. Use a quality lock for your bicycle and don't leave valuables in your car.

Travel in Groups

There's safety in numbers. When going out at night or to unfamiliar places, try to go with friends or family.

Be Cautious with Public Wi-Fi

Avoid accessing sensitive information when connected to public networks. Use a VPN for added security.

Use Strong, Unique Passwords

Create complex passwords and use a password manager. Enable two-factor authentication whenever possible.

Verify Before Allowing Entry

Always check identification of service personnel before allowing them into your home. Don't hesitate to call the company to verify.

Trust Your Instincts

If a situation or person makes you feel uncomfortable, remove yourself from it. Your intuition is a powerful safety tool.
